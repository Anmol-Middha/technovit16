SHERLOCK OHMS

7 Apr 5 min read

“My mind rebels at stagnation, give me problems, give me work!”

Get ready, put on your hats, and for heaven’s sake, Watson, get over here! Sherlock Ohms is no ordinary game… in fact “it’s quite a three pipe problem”. This event is designed to test your quick-thinking, observation, as well as technical skills. Play a Sherlock Holmes starring in an electrical world, and solve riddles and crimes to win."The question is not if, but how. The Game is afoot.”

RULES AND ABOUT THE GAME

As Holmes and Watson, your duet team shall endure 4 rounds, two hours for two days.

Day 1 comprises of 2 rounds. The first sees each group given a set of brain twisters to be solved in the given time, succeeding which, you proceed to round 2. In the second round, a stimulating treasure hunt for ‘data’ (components) is held, before the strike of the clock. Failure to do so will result in elimination.

Day 2 begins with round 3, and your eye through a magnifying glass. Yes, this round shows Holmes and Watson working a real time case, interrogating witnesses, and observing clues, from which they must solve the case. To take a leaf out of the detective’s book, “The little details are by far the most important”. In the fourth and final round, the detectives are given fake currency, to ‘buy’ the components, according to the given list (price and points of components mentioned), from volunteers located around the campus. In case more than one detective requests to buy the component, an auction will be started. Be wary, the team with most points and highest savings of money will be declared the winner. To all those investigators out there, “come at once if convenient. If inconvenient, come all the same”.

MATH-E- TRONICS

6 Apr 2 min read

As we move forward towards new technology and inventions, and with the idea of smart city driving us to a better tomorrow, there is a need to learn a smart way to design our circuits to meet their needs.

For those aspiring for circuit designing, but have no clue where to start, Mathetronics is your place to be. A great way to stay “connected’, it is a two day tutorial class workshop, the first day being about the basics of circuit design, and the second an on-screening and team selection (based on a 3 round competition).

Teams of two will go through a three questionnaire rounds, the first two being 10 questions in 30 minutes. The last round will have 10 questions for a time period of 60 minutes. Selection of the team will be based on successfully solving of the problem in the shortest time period. Periodic eliminations will take place between the rounds, sending only half its competitors onwards, and 4 teams for the final round.

DEATH RACE v2.0

< 6 Apr 2 min read

Do YOU have the world’s most DEADLY robot? Are YOU ready to CRUSH YOUR OPPONENT? Are you READY TO WIN, FIGHT till the end, fight till DEATH? If that’s so, YOU are in the right place. Welcome to connectiVITieee’16‘s very own DEATH RACE! Brace yourselves to experience the ultimate battle of blades, chainsaws, lasers, and mighty pushes, a war of brains and brawn, and robot possessed devils themselves. This time, we’re taking the Death Race to a whole new level! Don’t say we didn’t warn you.

ON THE BATTLEGROUND
The Death Race comprises of two levels of competition. The first, themed as ‘Fast & Furious, is a RoboRace. The bot must overcome the various hurdles within the time-limit and reach the final checkpoint. Teams may choose to skip a hurdle at the cost of obtaining a penalty. The second, is themed as RoboWar. Every team’s robot must bear a minimum of one weapon. Explosive weapons are not allowed. This is a point-based level. The winner will be decided based on scores from both the levels.

SCORE STRUCTURE
Level 1:
Fast & Furious: The maximum Time limit to complete the track is 3 mins. The scoring will be done in the following way: (3*60 seconds – Time taken by the Robot to complete the track (in sec)). (Weapons are not allowed in this level.)
Level 2:
The RoboWar: Two teams will compete in the pit for 1.5 minutes. 10 points will be awarded when one robot hits the other. If a team disables the robot of the other then 20 bonus points will be awarded. If a team pushes another robot out of the pit then 20 points will be awarded.
Grand Finale:
Best teams (combined success in all the above levels) will compete in the real Death Race. Two teams will race together and points will be awarded for damaging the other bot while racing. The bots will then cross the war pit where they will have to fight for 1.5 minutes, and then continue the race. The team surpassing all the levels will thus be named the victor of the Death Race.

RULES AND REGULATIONS
Teams:

  1. Teams of 4 or 5 can register, each team having a unique name (team name and robot name may be the same).
  2. In case of repeated team names by more than one team, teams may require a name change before proceeding for the competition. Organizers must immediately be notified of any name changes, failing which, the team will face disqualification.
  3. Team captain (compulsory) must ensure smooth communication between the organizers and the participating teams. All the important dates, updates in the rules and all the queries will be communicated with the respective captain. The captain must submit his/her email id and contact number at the time of registration.


Theme:
Death race is the brutal and breath-taking combination of Robo-Racing and Robo-Wars. Teams will compete two at a time. Respective teams will place their bots in the arena. Teams have to transverse the whole path while destroying their opponent’s robot.

Arena and Game:
The game field is strewn with obstacles such as sand, dust, mud, pebbles, slopes and watery surfaces. The track will contain curves, straight paths as well as slopes. Robots are allowed to damage the other bot or to push the opponent bot out of the track. The length of the arena track will be disclosed at the time of competition. The track will have checkpoints at regular intervals, at which judges will grade the bot.

Robot Specifications:
  1. The robot dimensions MUST NOT EXCEED 500x500x400 mm cuboid at any time during the competition. If it exceeds the above dimensions then the respective team will be disqualified immediately.
  2. The maintenance parts such as battery, wheels, communication setup or pressure bottles, must be easily accessible.
  3. The weapon and battery setup must be easily removable and replaceable within a span of 5 minutes (in case of emergency).
  4. Robots can be controlled wirelessly or by wire for example Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, radio, etc.
  5. Other than the communication protocol, the maximum number of sensors that can be mounted on the robot is 2.
  6. Except for flame-throwers, teams are free to choose any weapon to mount on their bot. Weapons including projectile are not allowed. Lasers on the robot, if any, must not exceed Class 2. Hidden weapons on the robot will result in immediate disqualification.
  7. Jumping and flying robots are not allowed.
  8. It is the responsibility of the participating teams to take care there is no interference of frequencies. Such a circumstance will not be considered for a rematch.

Powers Source:
  1. Robot must be powered only electrically. Any other power sources will lead to disqualification.
  2. The power source of the robot must not increase beyond 24V at any point of the race. The power source check will be done at any time and for any number of times during the event.
  3. Teams are not allowed to change their batteries at any point of time during the race, but may do so during their time-out.
  4. The robots must have an emergency switch to avoid any mishaps during the race.

Point System:
  1. One race will go on for a maximum of ten minutes (Death race final event).
  2. 20 points will be deducted every time the bot moves out of the track.
  3. 50 points will be awarded for crossing a checkpoint.
  4. 10 points will be deducted for rule violation.
  5. Each team can take a time out of 10 seconds. In that time, team members can enter the arena and touch their bot.
  6. Points will also be awarded for aggression, stability, control as well as innovative ideas.

Violations:
  1. Team member cannot touch their robot during the race.
  2. The size of the robot should not exceed the dimensions mentioned.
  3. Any de-motivational or harsh language will be considered as violation.
  4. Any deceiving method like hidden weapons or lasers, etc. used will be treated as violation of rules.

Event Co-ordinators:

Event Description:

The event is a race event where the fastest line follower bot will be awarded the first prize. It consists of two rounds where in each team is given single try in each round.
The first round is before lunch and the next will be after the lunch.
Each team may consist maximum of 4 members.

Rules:

  1. The winner will be decided on who completes the track fastest. The track’s image is given below.
  2. Track width is 30mm. The starting point and the finishing points are represented as ‘S’ and ‘F’.
  3. Each team has a balance of 50 points (subject to change). These points will be considered in case when there is a tie.
  4. Track consists of two checkpoints. They are represented as ‘C1’ and ‘C2’. The bots must pass through C1 and C2. Crossing these checkpoints would result in awarding of 10 points.
  5. The race will be considered as a tie if the time taken by two or more teams is same (highly unlikely), or if the team is not able to complete it in the allotted time (8 mins).
  6. The points will be deducted on these basis:
    • If the bot goes offline – 5 points
    • Per touch – 2 points
    • If the bot takes wrong path – 7 points
  7. In case of tie, the above criteria will follow and the winner will be the one with more points.
  8. The path required to be followed is marked by arrows.
